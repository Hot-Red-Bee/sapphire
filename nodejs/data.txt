Nelson Mandela (1918-2013) was a South African anti-apartheid revolutionary and political leader who became the country's first Black president (1994-1999).
 Born in Mvezo, South Africa, he joined the African National Congress in 1944 and fought against apartheid. He was imprisoned for 27 years (1962-1990) for 
 opposing the racist apartheid system. After his release, 
 he led South Africa's transition from apartheid to democracy, 
 promoting racial reconciliation. He won the Nobel Peace Prize in 1993
